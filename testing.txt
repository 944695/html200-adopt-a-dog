Adopt a Dog - User Experience Testing
Date: 2026-06-15
Project: HTML200 Adopt a Dog

User Personas
- Persona 1: Busy Parent (Age 35-50)
  - Goal: Find a family-friendly dog quickly and understand adoption steps.
  - Behavior: Scans pages for key info, prefers clear navigation and short content blocks.
  - Likely devices: iPhone/Android phone during the day, laptop at home.

- Persona 2: College Student (Age 18-24)
  - Goal: Compare available dogs and read practical care tips before adopting.
  - Behavior: Browses many cards and images, uses social apps and web links heavily.
  - Likely devices: Android phone and tablet.

- Persona 3: Retired Adult (Age 60+)
  - Goal: Adopt a companion dog and complete inquiry form confidently.
  - Behavior: Reads carefully, values larger text and obvious form labels.
  - Likely devices: Desktop browser (Windows/Mac), occasionally iPad.

Primary Device Assumptions
- Mobile-first behavior for discovery (quick browsing of dogs and blog posts).
- Desktop/laptop behavior for longer reading and form completion.
- Mixed network quality, so pages should load with simple, clean HTML structure.

Pages Tested
- index.html (Home)
- dogs.html (All Dogs)
- blog.html (Blog)
- checkout.html (Dog profile + adoption form)
